September 15, 2024

Fashionistas on social media have already declared the biker boot the “must-wear” shoe of the season. 

The durable style will help keep your feet warm and avoid dreaded wet socks, and looks great with everything from a skirt and woolly tights to jeans. 

And given that everyone from Hailey Bieber to Bella Hadid have rocked the chunky style of footgear, you can bet that as the weather turns they’ll be flying off the shelves. 

But while the favourite pair of celebs – Miu Miu’s Buckled LeatherBoots – would set you back a staggering £1.3k, savvy shoppers have found a far more affordable alternative set that looks every bit as modish. 

H&M’s £54.99 Biker Boots are incredibly similar to the designer offering – they’re also sturdy with a chunky heel, and have near-identical steel decorative tabs around the ankle. 

The only real difference is that H&M’s version are faux leather – and some £1,240 cheaper.

Savvy shoppers took to TikTok to share their disbelief at just how dead-on the high-street dupes are, with one user, @Shaannonsaanders, racking up 79k views on a video showing her pair off. 

“These are exactly what I was looking for, yay,” one user commented. 

In another clip, Shannon showed off just how versatile the boots are, styling them with everything from baggy jeans to a long lace skirt. 

They’re a “little stiff” at first, she added, “but they feel pretty durable”.

Plenty of users are also raving about the pair in H&M's review section, with one writing they’re the “perfect fit and they’re even nicer than the photo”. 

You can pick a pair up online or in store – but it might be worth trying on or sizing up, as some customers have noticed that the top can be rather tight around the calves. 

And while you’re there, you might want to nab some of the other H&M dupes that have been sending shoppers into a frenzy. 

There’s a super-similar version of Gucci’s £2,200 Jackie Shoulder Bag, which you can pick up for just £27.99. 

The high-street store's SKIMS dupes have also been impressing shoppers, with figure-hugging neutral T-shirts available for just £3.50 a pop.
