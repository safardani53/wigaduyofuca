Newcastle flop and World Cup winner Stephane Guivarc'h quits football to sell swimming pools

September 30, 2024

NEWCASTLE flop Stephane Guivarc'h has plunged into a new career... selling SWIMMING POOLS.

Toon boss Kenny Dalglish paid Auxerre £3.5million for the World Cup-winning France striker in 1998.

Guivarc'h made just four appearances, scoring once, before new manager Ruud Gullit flogged him to Rangers that November.

But now the 47-year-old is making a splash in his new job after teaming up with a boyhood friend who runs a pool business.

And Guivarc'h is driving round Brittany every day to promote their products.

The Frenchman told Le Parisien: "I could have stayed in football.  I was Guingamp sporting director for six months, but fell out with their president.

"My mum then died, and I looked after my dad while I had my house built.

"My childhood friend - who is now my boss - was fitting a water tank one day when I jokingly told him I could be a salesman for his swimming pool firm.
