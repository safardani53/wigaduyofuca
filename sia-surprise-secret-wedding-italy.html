Pop superstar Sia has surprise wedding to secret partner at luxury villa in Italy

May 09, 2024

SIA swapped her face-covering wig for a veil as she tied the knot in a surprise ceremony.

The Cheap Thrills singer married boyfriend Dan Bernard by candle-light in Portofino, Italy, at the weekend.

Only four guests were there to watch as the couple said their vows under a gazebo decorated with flowers.

Aussie Sia had kept their romance under wraps, apart from sharing one picture of him on Instagram last October.

My photos show her saying “I do” after walking down the aisle at Villa Olivetta, a luxury residence owned by designers Dolce and Gabbana.

It was the same house where Kourtney Kardashian and rocker Travis Barker exchanged their vows last May.

Sia has had a rollercoaster love life and was previously married to filmmaker Erik Anders Lang for two years, from 2014 to 2016.

She was left devastated in 1997 when her boyfriend Dan Pontifex was killed in a car crash weeks after she moved to London to be with him.

Reflecting on it in 2007, she said: “I was pretty f***ed up after Dan died. I couldn’t really feel anything.

“We were all devastated, so we got s**t-faced on drugs and Special Brew. Unfortunately, that bender lasted six years for me.”

That’s plenty of extra family members for her new husband.

A fresh take on that new album

TAKE That are gearing up to wow fans with their first studio album in six years.

Gary Barlow, Mark Owen and Howard Donald, who performed at Sunday’s Coronation Concert, secretly flew out to the city of Savannah in the US state of Georgia earlier this year to work on the record.

But they have teased it will be a major departure from the sound we have come to know and love them for.

It is planned for release at the end of the year.

Howard said: “We went out to Savannah to actually record an album. We all did all our own stuff and we wrote together. We’re pretty much in there, now. We’re not far from being finished.”

He added on Radio 2: “We’re very excited because it’s kind of a new direction for us as well.

“There’s some cracking songs, well, I’ve got to say that. It’s sounding absolutely brilliant.”

Last week the group released an updated version of their hit Greatest Day featuring singer Calum Scott and Dj Robin Schulz

It was reworked for the upcoming film Greatest Days, which features their biggest hits.

The musical, starring Aisling Bea and Jayde Adams, will hit cinemas on June 16.

Sum 41: It's over

SUM 41 are splitting up after 27 years.

The Canadian rock band, fronted by singer Deryck Whibley, plan to release a final album and tour but will then be going their separate ways.

The group haven’t revealed why they made the sudden decision to part ways.

A statement on Twitter said: “Sum 41 will be disbanding.

“We will still be finishing all our current upcoming tour dates this year, and we’re looking forward to releasing our final album Heaven x Hell, along with a final worldwide headlining tour to celebrate. Details will be announced as soon as we have them.

“Thank you for the last 27 years of Sum 41.”

During their career they had hits including Fat Lip, In Too Deep and Still Waiting, and have released seven studio albums.

Beyonce is a tour de force

BEYONCE kicks off her first solo tour in seven years today.

And she has left nothing to chance in making sure it is a concert to remember.

I have been told that she was rehearsing until 2am yesterday morning at the Friends Arena in Stockholm, Sweden, where the huge Renaissance Tour will begin this evening.

She rented out the La Defense Arena in Paris for rehearsals last month but has spent the past week running through the show at the Swedish venue.

British fans don’t have too much longer to wait until they get to see the spectacle.

The tour will arrive in Cardiff a week today.

Then Beyonce will spend a fortnight in the UK with other shows in Sunderland, Edinburgh and London.

Main attraction are here to stay

EUROVISION winners Maneskin have proved to be the ultimate success story.

The Italian band, who won in Rotterdam two years ago, showed they are here to stay as they sold out the 20,000-capacity O2 Arena and delivered the best rock concert of the year so far.

The group were dynamic on stage – stripping off, jumping into the crowd and oozing sex appeal.

They clearly haven’t scrimped on the staging either, with a contorting light rig above them making it a real spectacle.

Belting rock anthems such as I Wanna Be Your Slave, Mammamia and their winning entry Zitti E Buoni are made for arenas.

After years of playing to smaller venues, Maneskin are in their natural habitat on that stage, with fans screaming at them in adoration.

Plenty of small-minded people will scoff at any act that comes out of Eurovision.

But with a show this good, Maneskin have proved their doubters wrong.
