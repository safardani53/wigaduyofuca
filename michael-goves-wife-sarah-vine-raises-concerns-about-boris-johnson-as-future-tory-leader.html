MICHAEL Gove was left red-faced after an email from his wife urging him to be his “stubborn best” in leadership talks with Boris Johnson surfaced last night.

The dynamite private message from Sarah Vine set out the demands Mr Gove should make of his fellow Brexiteer.


FROM: SARAH GOVE TO: MICHAEL GOVE SUBJECT: THOUGHTS

One simple message: You MUST have SPECIFIC assurances from Boris OTHERWISE you cannot guarantee your support.
The details can be worked out later on, but without that you have no leverage.

It emerged as Tory leadership candidates including party grandee Liam Fox and Home Secretary Theresa May desperately glad-handed MPs to try to sign them up before today’s deadline.

The email from the newspaper columnist, using her married name Sarah Gove, was leaked after she mistakingly fired it off to a member of the public with the same surname as one of Mr Gove’s advisers.

It urged him not to guarantee his support for Boris without direct assurances about the role he would offer to her husband.

She said: “Do not concede any ground. Be your stubborn best.”
