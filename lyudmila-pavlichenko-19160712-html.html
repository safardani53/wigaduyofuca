Lyudmila Mikhailovna Pavlichenko, a Soviet sniper in the Red Army during World War II who recorded 309 confirmed casualties, making her the most successful sniper in recorded history. . Pavlichenko has been nicknamed the "Lady of the Dead" because of her incredible ability with a sniper rifle. She served in the Red Army during the Siege of Odessa and the Siege of Sevastopol, in the early stages of the fighting on the Eastern Front. After being injured by a mortar, she was evacuated to Moscow. After recovering from her injuries, she trained other Red Army snipers and was the Red Army's public spokesperson. In 1942, she toured the United States, Canada and England. After the war ended in 1945, she was appointed Senior Researcher for the Soviet Navy. She died of a stroke at the age of 58 .
