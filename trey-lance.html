Lance went to Marshall High School. He was one of the most suitable quarterbacks in town.

Lance was chosen as a quarterback by the colleges in the Group of Five conferences or the Football Championship Subdivision. Eventually, he went to North Dakota State in December 2017.

College Career

Lance played two games in the 2018 season and had 2 touchdowns. He could play limited games in the season. 

In 2019, Lance started the game, and his team entered the 2020 NCAA Division I Football Championship Game. He was the MVP of the game with a 28-20 win. 

Lance passed the ball 287 times for 28 touchdowns and zero interceptions in the season’s final game. Zero interceptions with 287 passes was an NCAA record. He ran 1,100 yards and 14 touchdowns in that season. 

Trey received the Walter Payton Award as the FCS’s most outstanding offensive player and the Jerry Rice Award as the top NCAA FCS player.

The 2020 season was delayed due to the global pandemic. As a result, Lance could only play one game in October 2020. Lance passed for 2 touchdowns and 1 interception in the match against Central Arkansas.

Lance began to prepare for the 2021 NFL Draft from the beginning of 2021 and left the games of Bison. 

Professional Career

The NFL Draft scouts and experts assumed that Lance would be the third overall pick of the San Francisco 49ers. 

The 49ers drafted him in the 2021 NFL Draft as the third pick in the first round. Lance was among the top 5 picks of the first round.

On July 28, 2021, Lance signed a four-year contract with the San Francisco 49ers. The deal was worth $34.1 million, including a signing bonus of $22.2 million.
