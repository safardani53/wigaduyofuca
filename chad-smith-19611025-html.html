Chad Gaylord Smith is an American musician, drummer for Red Hot Chili Peppers since 1988. The group was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2012. Smith is also the drummer for the hard rock supergroup. Chickenfoot, founded in 2008, and Chad Smith's Bombastic Meatbats musical ensemble, premiered in 2007. He has worked with The Chicks on Take the Long Way, an album that won 5 Grammy Awards in 2007. Smith has recorded with Glenn Hughes, Johnny Cash, John Fogerty, Jennifer Nettles, Kid Rock, Jake Bugg, Avett. Brothers, Joe Satriani, Dang Malone, Lana Del Rey and Halsey. In 2010, starring Dick Van Dyke and Leslie Bixler, he released Rhythm Train, a children's album featuring Smith singing and playing various musical instruments. In 2020, Smith co-wrote and performed with the support group on Ozzy Osbourne's Ordinary Man album. Spin Magazine ranked Smith at number 10 on its May 2013 list of the "100 Greatest Alternate Drummers".
