Whitney Cummings looks unrecognizable with dark hair and RED eyes in creepy red carpet turn at Studi

October 09, 2024
blog

WHITNEY Cummings looked completely unrecognizable with a creepy red carpet look of dark hair and red eyes at the Studio 666 movie premiere.

The 39-year-old comedian, who attended the star-studded event on Wednesday in Los Angeles, stars alongside the Foo Fighters in the comedy-horror flick.

Switching things up from her recent bright hair, Whitney dyed her hair dark to celebrate the upcoming release of her new movie.

Recently, she’d been seen with blonde, orange, and pink hair – changing it up frequently.

On social media, the actress likes to show off her bare, makeup-free face, but she glammed it up for the red carpet.

Whitney had on foundation, some nice rouge on her cheeks, and a deep red lip.

The red didn’t stop there, though, as she also wore red contacts, giving her whole look an extra spooky feel.

Keeping the theme going, she wore a blood red dress with black animal print, capping up the look with a pair of strappy black heels.

According to the film’s website, in Studio 666: “Rock & Roll Hall of Famers Foo Fighters move into an Encino mansion steeped in grisly rock history to record their much anticipated 10th album. 

“Once in the house, Dave Grohl finds himself grappling with supernatural forces that threaten both the completion of the album and the lives of the band.”

After forming in 1994 and having almost 30 years of various forms of content together as a band, the Foo Fighters decided it was time to switch things up; thus, a movie.

Lead singer Dave Grohl said in a press release, per Deadline: “After decades of ridiculous music videos and numerous music documentaries under our collective belts, it was finally time to take it to the next level… A full length feature horror comedy film.

“Like most things Foo, Studio 666 began with a far fetched idea that blossomed into something bigger than we ever imagined possible. 

“Filmed at the same house where we recorded our latest album Medicine at Midnight — told you that place was haunted! — we wanted to recapture the classic magic that all of our favorite rock and roll movies had, but with a twist: hilarious gore that fucking rocks.” 

He continued: “And now, with the help of Tom Ortenberg and the team at Open Road Films we can finally let this cat out of the bag after keeping it our best kept secret for two years. 

“Be ready to laugh, scream, and headbang in your popcorn. Studio 666 will f**k you up.”

Along with Whitney and the Foo members, the movie also stars Leslie Grossman, Will Forte, Jenna Ortega, and Jeff Garlin.

The flick heads to theaters for one night only on February 25.

Along with her new movie, Whitney also has her Touch Me Tour comedy tour, which kicked off last year with a surprise appearance by Bachelorette alum Katie Thurston.

Around the time of starting her tour, Whitney also helped filled in on The Wendy Williams Show as a guest host in October.

She temporarily took over the hosting duties during the week of Monday, October 25 through Friday, October 29.  

Ahead of the gig, Whitney said in a press release: “In my opinion, nobody is more entertaining or authentic than Wendy Williams.

“As an avid fan, it is surreal to sit in for Wendy, but as someone who has been a guest on the show so many times, I feel confident that my relentless habit of speaking candidly, often to the point of self-sabotage, may go over well with her fans.”
