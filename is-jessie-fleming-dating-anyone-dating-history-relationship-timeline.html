Jessie Fleming’s Family and Educational History

Jessie Fleming was born on March 11, 1998, in Ontario, Canada. Her father, John Fleming, is from Toronto, as is her mother, Michaele Fleming. She has two siblings: Tristan, her elder brother, and Elysse, her younger sister. She used to play track and field, cross-country running, full-contact boys’ hockey, soccer, and other sports as a kid. Furthermore, Jessie started playing soccer at the age of three and played with the Nor’West Optimist Soccer Club in London till 2016.

Fleming had her primary schooling at Ryerson Public School, where she won every cross-country competition she competed in. Later, from years 9 to 10, she attended London Central Secondary School, where she competed in cross-country and OFSAA track and field. In addition, the player won the 4000-meter novice girls’ event at the OFSAA Cross Country Championships in 2012. Jessie also moved to H. B. Beal Secondary School for Grades 11 and 12, where she graduated in 2016.

Jessie Fleming’s Professional Life

Jessie Fleming began playing at an early age, as we all know. She did, however, formally pledge to UCLA in December 2014 and joined the Bruins in August 2016. The football player played in her debut game on August 28 and scored twice in a 4-3 defeat to Florida, less than ten days after helping Canada earn the bronze medal at the Olympic Games.

Fleming was also named to the NSCAA All-America third team in 2016, making her one of just two rookies to do so. She was selected to the NSCAA All-Pacific Region, All-Pac-12, and All-Freshman teams, as well as the Top Drawer Soccer Freshman of the Year. Jessie also had a 20-point season as a second-year player, with six goals, three game-winners, and eight assists. She was also named a finalist for the Hermann Trophy after scoring a goal and assisting UCLA in reaching the College Cup final.

Fleming was also named as one of four nominees for the Honda Sports Award for soccer’s Class of 2018. The professional soccer player graduated from UCLA in 2021 with a major in Materials Engineering and a minor in Environmental Science. Fleming also signed a three-year deal with FA WSL champion Chelsea on July 22, 2020, and made her Blues debut as a substitute on August 29 against Manchester City in the 2020 Women’s FA Community Shield at Wembley.
